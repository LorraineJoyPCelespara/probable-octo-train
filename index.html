Summer Holiday

Camp John Hay

Baguio, Philippines

A former military facility, Camp John Hay draws golfers to the Jack Nicklaus-designed course. Pine trees line the Eco Trail, providing shade to joggers and hikers, while Tree Top Adventure caters to thrill-seekers with ziplining courses.

Garin Farm

San Joaquin, Iloilo, Philippines

Situated at the southernmost town of the Province of Iloilo, Garin Farm is an inland resort that integrates agriculture, leisure and religion to create a farm, a recreational place and a pilgrimage site all in one location.

Fort Santiago

Manila, Philippines

Fort Santiago, built in 1593, is a citadel built by Spanish navigator and governor Miguel López de Legazpi for the new established city of Manila in the Philippines. The defense fortress is located in Intramuros, the walled city of Manila and is one of the most important historical sites in the said city.

Onok Island

Balabac, Palawan, Philippines

Onok Island is a tiny piece of paradise in the south of Palawan that features expansive shallow clear waters, giant clams, a sand bar appearing during low tide, wild sea turtles, a coral reef, and a stilt over-water walkway.

Cebu Taoist Temple

Cebu City, Cebu, Philippines

The Cebu Taoist Temple was built in 1972 by the Cebu’s substantial Chinese community. It is composed of two different temples: one is the Phu Sian Temple, built by another Chinese community, not open to the public; another is the main temple which is open to the worshipers and non-worshipers alike.

Vigan City

Vigan City, Philippines

Vigan is a city in the Philippines, on the west coast of Luzon island. It's known for its preserved Spanish colonial and Asian architecture. Near the white baroque Vigan Cathedral are Plaza Salcedo, offering fountain light shows, and Plaza Burgos, known for its street-food stalls.
